# The Armor of God — An Interactive Study Guide

A self-contained, interactive study of **Ephesians 6:10–20** — built for personal faith growth, family Bible study, and deeper engagement with Scripture.

## What's Inside

This guide walks through Paul's teaching on spiritual warfare and the believer's equipment in Christ. It's designed to work as a devotional, a teaching resource, or a self-study tool.

- **The Full Passage** — Ephesians 6:10–20 displayed as a parchment scroll with the armor phrases highlighted
- **The Six Pieces of Armor** — interactive cards for the Belt of Truth, Breastplate of Righteousness, Shoes of the Gospel, Shield of Faith, Helmet of Salvation, and Sword of the Spirit. Each opens a study panel with:
  - The ancient Roman picture
  - The spiritual reality
  - Cross-references
  - A reflection question
- **Study Tools** — curated links to Bible Gateway, Blue Letter Bible, Bible Hub, Enduring Word, Matthew Henry, and Got Questions
- **Deep Study Section** — expandable panels on authorship, Roman military imagery, the meaning of "stand firm," who the real enemy is, the role of prayer, and defensive vs. offensive weapons
- **Three Progressive Quizzes:**
  - **Foundational** — the basics: what each piece is and represents
  - **Intermediate** — theology, context, and application
  - **Advanced / Seminary Level** — Greek terminology, Old Testament roots, exegetical depth
- **Reflection Questions** — five questions for personal or group application

## How to Use It

Just open the live link in any browser — phone, tablet, or computer. No account, no login, nothing to install.

**On mobile:** Tap your browser's Share button → **Add to Home Screen**. It'll act like an app.

**For personal study:** Work through the sections in order, or jump to the armor piece or quiz that's most relevant to where you are.

**For family or small group study:** Go through one piece of armor per session, discuss the reflection question, and end with prayer.

## Technical Notes

- Single HTML file — all CSS, JavaScript, and graphics are self-contained
- No backend, no database, no tracking
- No external dependencies except Google Fonts
- Works offline once loaded
- Quiz progress does not persist between sessions (closing the tab resets it)
- Fully responsive — built to work well on mobile
